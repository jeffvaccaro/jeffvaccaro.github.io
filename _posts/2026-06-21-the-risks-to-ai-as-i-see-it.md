AI is changing how we build, write, and solve problems, but it is also creating new risks that are easy to underestimate.

In this post, I want to break down the biggest risks I see right now and how we can approach them with practical guardrails.

## 1. Overtrust in Confident Output

Models can sound certain even when they are wrong. The risk is not just bad answers. The deeper risk is teams skipping verification because the output "looks right."

## 2. Knowledge Atrophy

If we offload too much thinking, we can lose core skills over time. The right goal is augmentation, not replacement of critical reasoning.

## 3. Security and Data Leakage

Sensitive data can leak through prompts, logs, or connected tools. Controls need to be intentional, not optional.

## 4. Hidden Bias and Uneven Impact

AI systems can amplify existing bias in subtle ways. That impact is often felt first by users with the least room for error.

## 5. Accountability Gaps

When AI participates in decisions, ownership can get blurry. "The model said so" cannot be the final answer for responsibility.

The opportunity with AI is still massive, but long-term value depends on disciplined use, clear standards, and human accountability.
